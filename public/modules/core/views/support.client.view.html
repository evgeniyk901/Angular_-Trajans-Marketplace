Features of bitcoin

  • Transactions are near instantaneous and borderless – Bitcoin can be transferred from Australia to China in 10 minutes. There is no bank to slow down the process, level outrageous fees, or freeze the transfer. You can pay a friend across the dinning table at a restaurant when splitting the bill the same way as you can pay for the purchase of an item in another country.
  • Zero or minimal fees – Bitcoin's high cryptographic security allows it to process transactions in a very efficient and inexpensive way. You can make and receive payments using the Bitcoin network with almost no fees. In most cases, fees are not strictly required but they are recommended for faster confirmation of your transaction.
  • No risk of charge-backs (its irreversible) - Any business that accepts credit cards or PayPal knows the problem of payments that are later reversed. Charge-back frauds result in limited market reach and increased prices, which in turn penalises customers. Bitcoin payments are irreversible and secure, meaning that the cost of fraud is no longer pushed onto the shoulders of the merchants.
  • It is secure - Bitcoin transactions are secured by military grade cryptography. Nobody can charge you money or make a payment on your behalf. So long as you take the required steps to protect your wallet , Bitcoin can give you control over your money and a strong level of protection against many types of fraud.
  • Protect your identity - With Bitcoin, there is no credit card number that some malicious actor can collect in order to impersonate you. In fact, it is even possible to send a payment without revealing your identity, almost just like with physical money. You should however take note that some effort can be required to protect your privacy.
  • Expand your market - Bitcoin is an emerging market of new customers who are searching for ways to spend their bitcoins. Accepting them is a good way to get new customers and give your business some new visibility. Accepting a new payment method has often shown to be a clever practice for online businesses.
  • It is deflationary – Only 21 million bitcoin will ever be created under the original specification. This means that after that, the number of bitcoins won’t grow, so inflation won’t be a problem, in fact, deflation – where the price of goods and services potentially fall.
  • It represents freedom (its decentralised!) – Because bitcoin is decentralised, it represents a new level of independence, and freedom. Bitcoin allows individuals and businesses alike, sovereignty over their own money.

What is bitcoin?

Bitcoin – money for the internet, is a decentralised digital currency that enables fast, secure and low cost payments to anyone, anywhere in the world. Bitcoin uses peer-to-peer technology to operate with no central authority; managing transactions and issuing money are carried out collectively by the network. Bitcoin allows individuals sovereignty over their own money. Conceived by Satoshi Nakamoto in 2008 and introduced to the world in 2009, bitcoin is one of the first successful implementations of a distributed crypto-currency. Bitcoin is designed around the idea of using cryptography to control the creation and transfer of money, rather than relying on central authorities. The current market capitalisation of bitcoin is 5.12 billion AUD (19/03/15). Further information of bitcoin can be found at www.bitcoin.org

How do I become an ID Verified Merchant?

To begin the verification procedure visit the user dashboard http://www.ltclancer.com/#!/settings/verify You will need to provide two forms of identification.

  • 1. Government issued photo ID showing your full name and date of birth. The document must:
    • Be current (i.e. not expired).
    • Be in full colour.

Driver licence, proof of age card, and passport are good examples.

  • 2. A statement showing your full name and current residential address. The statement must:
    • Be current (i.e. issued in the last 3 months).
    • Be addressed to you and physically sent to your current residential address.
    • Not be addressed to a PO box. We may accept a statement sent to a PO box providing the residential address is listed clearly on the statement.
    • Not be electronically generated (e.g. sent to you as a PDF).

Utility bills, bank statements and tax statements are good examples. Additionally, if you're unable to provide a statement meeting the above conditions, your local bank branch should be able to print and stamp an address confirmation statement.

These documents must be scanned or photographed at high resolution, then uploaded separately under the appropriate category. You may watermark the image for security purposes providing the watermark does not obscure any of the elements described above. We may reject watermarked images at our discretion.

Your identification documents will be securely transferred only over Secure Socket Layer (SSL) and stored using bank-grade encryption.

Manual ID verification can take up to three business days. If you have not been identified within this time, or you wish to request that the process be expedited, please contact our support team.

What is escrow?

Escrow provides buyer's with an optional safety net when purchasing from unverified sellers. After the buyer has completed the checkout procedure, funds are temporarily transferred to a secure holding account, where they will be redistributed to the merchant on the buyers reception of the item. Trajans's optional secure escrow has been introduced to minimise fraud and ensure the quality and integrity of unverifed merchants are at the highest levels possible..

How do you secure escrow funds?

Escrow funds are transferred to our off-line wallets where they will be transferred to a cold wallet until the reception of the item is confirmed by the buyer.

How are escrow disputes resolved.

Trajan encourages merchants and buyers to resolve disputes with the counter-party;

Buyer initiates escrow refund
  • • Buyer does not receive item within 14 days. Buyer can request an escrow refund.
  • 1. Select User Dashboard, under the buying tab, select 'Get Help' button. Buyer will need to provide the reason for escrow refund request and submit any supporting evidence or documentation.
  • 2. Merchant will be emailed by Trajans and provided with the buyers reason for escrow refund request and any supporting evidence or documentation.
  • 3. Merchant will be given 72 hours to contact the buyer and resolve the issue or open a dispute.
    • Failing to reply within 72 hours may result in Trajans releasing funds to buyer.
    • Failing to resolve the issue with the buyer may result in Trajans releasing funds to buyer
    • Merchant providing proof of delivery may result in funds being released to merchant
Merchant initiates escrow refund
  • • If a buyer is not releasing funds within 14 days, merchant can request an escrow refund from Trajans.
  • • Buyer will be contacted by Trajans and given 72 hours to contact the merchant and resolve the issue or dispute the escrow payment.
    • Failing to reply within 72 hours may result in Trajans releasing funds to buyer.
    • Failing to resolve the issue with the merchant may result in Trajans releasing funds to merchant
    • Merchant providing proof of delivery may result in funds being released to merchant
Inactive escrow funds

If the buyer does not release escrow or raise a dispute and seller does not request a refund escrow will be auto released after 21 days.

How does Trajans determine a final solution?

In the event that a resolution is unsuccessful between a merchant and buyer, Trajans follows an impartial systematic approach to reach a final solution.

The key factors that determine our solutions are:
  • Evidence of purchased items being received by the buyer e.g. (tracking number, consignments note)
  • Does the purchased item have postal insurance
  • Prior transactions with Trajans including escrow
  • Visual evidence of any damage to the purchased item e.g. (photos, video)
  • Willingness of a merchant and seller to cooperate with our requests for information
  • Willingness of Merchant to accept returned item(s)
  • Verification of buyer and seller's details
